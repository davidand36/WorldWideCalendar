An international standard date notation, popular in Sweden and other European countries.

Published by the International Organization for Standardization (ISO) in 1988 C.E. as ISO 8601, which unified several earlier ISO standards. Based on the Gregorian calendar.

Dates are expressed as days, weeks, and years; months are not used. A year begins on the Monday of the week which includes 4 January (Gregorian).

Arithmetic solar calendar, because of its connection to the Gregorian calendar, but without the vaguely lunar month unit of the latter.

Epoch: Same as the Gregorian, Julian Day 1,721,426, which, conveniently, was a Monday.
